What is Rescal?

Rescal is a MATLAB program to calculate the resolution of a neutron triple-axis spectrometer. Matlab is linear algebra based and allows the resolution code to be written concisely and transparently. The Cooper- Nathans method is used to calculate the dimensions of the resolution ellipsoid. Spectrometer parameters such as the monochromator & analyser d- spacings are entered interactively. The program uses these to calculate

  • Bragg widths
  • Vanadium widths
  • Resolution ellipsoid projections
  • Phonon widths.

    A Monte-Carlo routine to simulate and fit inelastic neutron data is also under development. Results from the Rescal program have been compared with the results of programs used at other installations, and are in agreement.

    Parameters Window

      Parameters detailing the configuration of the spectrometer are required. These are entered interactively, or from a file. The parameters are labelled as follows:

      DM (mono. d-spacing in Ang.) DA (analyser d-spacing in Ang.)

      ETAM (mono. mosaic in min.) ETAA (analyser mos. in min.) ETAS (sample mos. in min.)
      SM (scattering sense from mono. +1=right, -1=left) SS (sample) SA (analyser)
      KFIX (fixed neutron wavevector in Ang.-1) FX (fixed wavevector; 1=incident 2=final)
      ALF1 (horizontal source-mono. collimation in mins.) ALF2 (horiz. mono.-sample coll.) ALF3 (horiz. sample-analyser coll.) ALF4 (horiz. analyser-detector coll.)
      BET1 (vertical source-mono. collimation in mins.) BET2 (vertical mono.-sample coll.) BET3 (vertical sample-analyser coll.) BET4 (vertical analyser-detector coll.)
      AS (sample latt. par. in Ang.) BS (latt. par. in Ang.) CS (latt. par. in Ang.)
      AA (angle in deg. between b and c) BB (between c and a) CC (between a and b)
      AX (r.l.u.) AY (r.l.u.) AZ (r.l.u.) First wavevector in scattering plane.
      BX (r.l.u.) BY (r.l.u.) BZ (r.l.u.) Second wavevector in scattering plane.
      QH (r.l.u.) QK (r.l.u.) QL (r.l.u.) EN (meV) Position of resolution wavevector in Q and E.
      DQH (r.l.u.) DQK (r.l.u.) DQL (r.l.u.) DEN (meV) Increment of Q,E defining general scan.
      GH (r.l.u.) GK (r.l.u.) GL (r.l.u.) GMOD (meVAng.) Direction of planar dispersion of slope GMOD.
